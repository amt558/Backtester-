LITE behavior change on Live cards: K-S divergence is still computed and shown, but a failure (p<0.01) shows a "REVIEW URGENT" badge instead of auto-flipping the card to disabled. Notify fires at severity=warning instead of severity=critical. The Disable button on the card stays clickable so you can act manually after looking at the data.

Why this matters: K-S on 30 trades is a noisy statistic; auto-disabling on a single bad p-value can mute a card during a regime transition that would self-correct. Trade-off: a card that's truly broken stays live for as long as it takes you to notice the REVIEW badge.

The honest read
Pick FULL if: you trust automated guardrails to act when you're not at the desk, you want maximum information density, and you accept that some signals (per-regime PF, K-S auto-disable) may fire on thin statistical samples.

Pick LITE if: you'd rather make every kill-decision yourself based on the data shown, you trust your own pattern recognition over machine-driven thresholds, and you want to see what the system says before letting it act.

Most realistic third option: ship LITE first (5 days), live with it for 30-60 days, then decide whether to add FULL's two extras (per-strategy regime fit + K-S auto-disable) based on what the calibration banner is telling you. Calibration tells you whether your judgment alone is enough; if it shows you're missing decay events the system should have caught, then you upgrade to FULL.
